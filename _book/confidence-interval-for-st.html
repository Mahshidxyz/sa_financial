2.2 Confidence interval for \(S(t)\)

For the contruction of the confidence interval we can use a well-know estimator of the variance, the Greenwood estimator. The Greenwood variance estimate for a Kaplan-Meier curve is defined as

\[ \hat \sigma^2[\hat S(t)] = \widehat var[\hat S(t)] = \hat S(t) \sum_{i:t_i \le t} \frac{d_i}{n_i(n_i-d_i)} \]

In case of no censoring, this estimator reduces to

\(\hat \sigma^2[\hat S(t)] = \frac{\hat S(t) [1- \hat S(t)]}{n}\).

It is possible to use this estimator to derive a confidence interval for all time points \(t\). Let \(\sigma\) denotes the greenwood’s standard deviation. Then confidence intervals for the survival function are then computed as follows (plain)

\[ \bigg(\hat S(t) \pm z_{1-\alpha/2} \cdot \hat \sigma \bigg), \] where $ = se(S(t))$ is calculated using greenwood’s formula.

It is important to hightlight here that this confidence interval may be out of the (0,1) interval! For solve this, the approximation to the normal distribution is improved by using the log-minus-log transformation

\[ \bigg(\hat S(t) \pm \exp^{z_{1-\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\hat\sigma}{\hat S(t) ln \hat S(t)}} \bigg), \].

Other options include the log transformation \[ \bigg( \exp ln(\hat S(t)) \pm \exp^{z_{1-\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\hat\sigma}{\hat S(t) ln \hat S(t)}} \bigg), \].
